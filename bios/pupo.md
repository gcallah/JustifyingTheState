
================

Spartaco Pupo (born 1974) is Associate Professor of History of Political Thought at the University of Calabria. His
research focuses primarily on modern and contemporary political thought, as well as theories of the state and public
administration. He has published extensively in these fields, serves on the editorial boards of several Italian journals
in intellectual and political history, and is a member of international scholarly societies, including the Hume Society
and the Eighteenth-Century Scottish Studies Society (ECSSS). His recent books include David Hume (2020), Lo scetticismo
politico (2020), and Amministrazione pubblica e relazioni umane (2024). He has also edited the Italian editions of
Michael Oakeshott’s Lectures in the History of Political Thought (2021) and Judith N. Shklar’s Storrs Lectures (2024),
as well as the English edition of Gentile’s Genesis and Structure of Society.
